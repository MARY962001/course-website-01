What is Immersive Technology?

üï∂Ô∏è Virtual Reality: Users get to interact through a simulated reality. Vision and hearing is simulated. The goal is to immerse into a virtual world and prevent from seeing the real world. VR is about 75% virtual and 25% real.

üì± Augmented Reality: Amplifies real-world surroundings adding virtual features that the user may interact with. Augmented reality doesn‚Äôt try to immerse and block out the real world - it overlays graphics and animation on top of real-world environments. AR is about 25% virtual and 75% real.

VR vs. AR in Real Estate

Comparison of VR & AR in Real Estate
Feature Virtual.Reality..VR.... Augmented.Reality..AR...
Experience Type Fully immersive 3D world Overlays digital elements on the real world
Device Requirement VR headset (Oculus, HTC Vive) Smartphone, tablet, or AR glasses
Use Case in Real Estate Virtual property tours, metaverse real estate Interactive property previews, furniture staging
Interactivity Users navigate a complete virtual environment Users interact with digital elements in real-world spaces
Best For Remote property viewing, pre-construction projects Home customization, on-site enhancements
User Engagement High immersion but requires dedicated hardware More accessible but less immersive
Cost & Implementation Higher setup cost, requires 3D modeling Lower cost, can be integrated with mobile apps
Challenges Need for VR hardware, motion sickness concerns Accuracy of overlays, dependency on camera quality
